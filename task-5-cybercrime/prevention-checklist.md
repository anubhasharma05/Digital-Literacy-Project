Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)

(1) Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking details with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or customer support. Legitimate services will never ask for     this information.
(2) Do not approve unknown payment requests on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Remember that entering your PIN always means you are sending money, not receiving it. (UPI Safety Tip)
(3) Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources, especially if someone claims you will “receive money” after scanning. (UPI Safety Tip)
(4) Use strong and unique passwords for all your accounts (email, social media, banking apps) and update them regularly.
(5) Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
(6) Be cautious of suspicious links and messages received via SMS, email, or social media. Do not click on unknown links or download attachments from unverified sources.
(7) Only download apps from trusted sources like the Google Play Store or Apple App Store, and check app reviews before installing.
(8) Log out from shared or public devices and avoid saving passwords on such systems.
(9) Limit the personal information you share online, such as phone number, address, or college details, as it can be misused for scams.
(10) Regularly monitor your bank transactions and account activity to quickly identify any unauthorized transactions.

Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime or notice suspicious activity:

(1) Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
(2) Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (for urgent financial fraud cases)

Quick reporting increases the chances of recovering lost money and preventing further damage.
